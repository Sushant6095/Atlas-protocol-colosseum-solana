Atlas /qvac — local-AI surfaces (Tether QVAC)

Four user-facing surfaces. The structured payload comes from Atlas. The plain-language explanation, the invoice OCR, the alert translation, and the second-opinion review run on your device. None of them touch the commitment path.

Privacy contract what runs locally vs server

local on your device

  • LLM that explains the pre-sign payload
  • OCR that extracts invoice fields
  • NMT that translates alerts and ledger rows
  • LLM + RAG that gives a second opinion on a pending bundle

from atlas structured data only

  • Pre-sign payload (projected balance, APY, fees, CU)
  • Pending bundle metadata + black-box record
  • Canonical English alert templates
  • Operator-confirmed invoice fields (image stays local)

The NMT runs only on cache miss. Identifiers (vault ids, hashes, addresses, numbers) must survive translation byte-for-byte; otherwise the output is rejected.

Approve + zero unrecognised concerns = "analyst clears" badge in the Squads queue. Anything else surfaces as escalate; the approver still signs (or doesn't) on their own judgment.

Out of scope deliberate non-uses

Picking integrations deliberately is a signal:

  • Replacing the deterministic MLP committed at vault creation (I-1) — the commitment-hash invariant rules this out.
  • Replacing the 7-agent ensemble — same reason.
  • SP1 proof generation on-device — devices lack the resources, prover registry slashing posture would be void.
  • Tether WDK custom wallets — Atlas is non-custodial; users connect existing wallets via wallet-standard.
  • STT voice commands for treasury operations — accidental-execution risk too high.
  • TTS for alerts — push notifications cover the audience.
